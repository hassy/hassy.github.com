h.h. veldstra - resumé, past projects &c

background

I am a generalist software developer with a product design background, and a proven ability to dive deep and solve challenging technical problems when needed.

I have worked on consumer webapps, RESTful APIs, compilers, web scrapers, applied machine learning, video transcoding and delivery, and dataviz projects using a variety of languages, frameworks and platforms.

I am interested in early-stage projects where I can wear multiple hats and where I can assume responsibility for an important part of the product. I am especially interested in user-facing work but I do enjoy working on backend services too.

tools

Python, Erlang, Javascript; Django, Flask; nginx; frontend dev with HTML5, CSS3, jQuery; D3; Redis, Postgres; Linux (Ubuntu) administration; AWS (EC2, S3, Route53); Heroku; probably some others I forget.

wonderwheel.fm

Vidiowiki

(2009-2012)

Lead developer for a video-based exploratory search engine with 100% original content and a mindmap-based search interface.

I worked on video uploading, transcoding and delivery (Python, ffmpeg, mencoder); a tool to allow users to create videos right on the site, including recording video with their webcams (Flex, JS); the user-facing Django-based web UI; and the visualization of relationships between videos (both UI and recommendations).

Vidiowiki raised a small seed round, and reached break-even point within 18 months of launch.

Hypernumbers (now Vixo)

(2007-2009)

Part of a team of three that developed a fully-functional online spreadsheet in Erlang in a year, with Excel file import, support for all of Excel's built-in functions, and features unique to Hypernumbers such as the ability to keep any cells in sync between any spreadsheets, full audit history, form creation, and collaboration features. (full list of Vixo's features)

I built the the formula engine (100% Excel compatibility, multilingual - en, de, fr, es, jp, ru, pt), a framework to automate testing of compatibility with Excel (tens of thousands of tests), and was responsible for i18n throughout the product.

Hypernumbers was one the winners of Seedcamp 2007.
